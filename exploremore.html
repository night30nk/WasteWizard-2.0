Understanding waste segregation is crucial in modern waste management. Biodegradable waste comprises organic materials that decompose naturally, such as food waste, leaves, and paper. On the other hand, non-biodegradable waste includes materials that do not decompose easily, like plastics, metals, and glass.
Proper separation of biodegradable and non-biodegradable waste facilitates recycling and effective disposal methods, contributing to a sustainable environment.
